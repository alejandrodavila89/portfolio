Overview

Modernizing a pharmacy giant's digital storefront

Farmacias del Ahorro is Mexico's largest pharmacy chain — over 1,700 stores, millions of monthly digital visitors, and a catalog of thousands of health and personal care SKUs. Despite that scale, their e-commerce platform was losing more than 70% of customers before checkout completion.

Working via Wizeline, I led the redesign of the end-to-end shopping experience — rebuilding the checkout flow, restructuring the product taxonomy, and establishing a design system to bring consistency across web, mobile, and CMS.

The problem
A multi-step checkout with high abandonment, confusing product categorization, and an interface built inconsistently across teams — making it hard for customers to find and buy what they needed.
The solution
A streamlined checkout, a restructured product taxonomy validated with real users, and a unified design system that brought visual and interaction consistency across the entire platform.

Research

Heuristic evaluation across web and mobile

Before touching a single wireframe, I ran a full heuristic evaluation across both platforms — mapping severity scores across 9 cognitive dimensions. The radar charts made it immediately clear to stakeholders where the platform was failing and why. This audit became the strategic brief for the entire redesign.

Information architecture

Restructuring the product taxonomy

The original catalog buried products under confusing categories. I rebuilt the taxonomy with card sorting and tree testing, so customers could reach medication and health products in fewer, clearer steps.

Checkout funnel

Turning a leaky checkout into a conversion path

I broke the checkout into its individual stages to see exactly where customers dropped off, then redesigned each step. Completion climbed from 30% to 65% across the funnel.

Process

From audit to a unified system

01 — Audit
Research & analytics
Audited the existing checkout and navigation, analyzed drop-off analytics, and ran usability sessions to pinpoint where and why customers abandoned their carts.
02 — Structure
Information architecture
Rebuilt the product taxonomy and navigation through card sorting and tree testing, making it faster for users to locate medication and health products.
03 — System
Design system & checkout
Designed a streamlined checkout flow and built a component library so the platform could scale consistently across web, mobile, and the CMS.

Results

A faster, clearer path to purchase

The redesigned platform lifted checkout completion from under 30% to 65% — more than doubling the conversion rate — while cutting navigation time to find products by 28%. The combined impact drove a 75% improvement in online sales performance for one of Mexico's largest pharmacy chains.
