Overview

A super-app for Mexico's underbanked population

BAZ is a fintech super-app by Banco Azteca targeting users with limited access to traditional banking. The platform offers loans, payments, and financial services through a single mobile experience.

When I joined via Wizeline, the app had significant friction in three core flows: loan application, user onboarding, and complaint reporting — leading to high drop-off rates and a surge in customer service cases.

My role was to research, redesign, and validate these flows end-to-end, working closely with product managers, engineers, and business stakeholders.

User journey

Mapping the loan journey end to end

I mapped the complete loan journey to expose where users hesitated, dropped off, or lost trust — turning each friction point into a clear design priority for the redesign.

Process

From research to high-fidelity

01 — Discover
Research & auditing
Analyzed existing flows, reviewed analytics data and NPS scores, and conducted 12 user interviews with BAZ customers across 3 Mexican cities to identify pain points.
02 — Define
Journey mapping & framing
Mapped the loan application journey, identified 7 critical drop-off points, and defined opportunity areas. Aligned with PMs and stakeholders on scope and success metrics.
03 — Design
Wireframes & iteration
Rapid wireframing in Figma, 3 rounds of usability testing, and progressive refinement to high-fidelity. Delivered annotated specs and a component library for engineering.

Measurable impact across all three flows
